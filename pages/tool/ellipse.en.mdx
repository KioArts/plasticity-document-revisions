# Ellipse

<Reference shortcut="" command="Ellipse" button="Toolbar" buttonIcon='button-center-circle' />

You can create a circle by specifying the center and diameter. 

To create an ellipse, start by clicking to define the center point. Then, drag outward to set the first radius (the major or minor axis), and click to confirm. Finally, drag perpendicular to the first radius to define the second radius, creating the ellipse shape. The two radii determine the width and height of the ellipse.

You can create an ellipse by specifying the center, radius of the major or minor axis.

## Basic Usage

1. Click the <ButtonIcon type="button-ellipse" /> button on the toolbar.
2. Specify the centre to begin drafting the radius of the major or minor axis.
3. Move the cursor to adjust the size of the ellipse and click to confirm.
   - <Kst>Keyboard Shortcuts</Kst>
      - <kbd>Tab</kbd>: Allows direct input of dimensions. For details, see [here](/tool/sketching-essentials#specify-dimensions-numerically).
      - <kbd>K</kbd> **Knife:** Please refer to [Using Knife Mode](/tool/sketching-essentials#use-knife-mode).
      - <kbd>X</kbd>/<kbd>Y</kbd> **X/Y-axis:** [Constrain to X, Y axis](/tool/sketching-essentials#constrain-to-an-axis).
4. Click OK in the command dialog or right-click to confirm.
5. The ellipse is created.
