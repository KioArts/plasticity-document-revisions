# Bridge

<Reference shortcut="" command="Bridge" button="" />

This command automatically calls the following command depending on the selected object.
- When two Curves are selected or nothing is selected: see [**"Bridge Curve"**](#bridge-curve) described below.
- When two Vertices are selected: see [**"Bridge Vertex"**](#bridge-vertex) described below.
- When two Edges are selected: see [**"Bridge Edge"**](#bridge-edge) described below.

## Bridge Curve[#bridge-curve]

You can connect two Curves while considering continuity. Additionally, you can connect them at any point on the edge, face, or construction plane.

<Callout emoji="❗">
    **Attention**<br />
Before selecting a point, please enable [**Grid Snaps**](../3d-viewport/grid-and-snap#grid-snap) and [**Object Snaps**](../3d-viewport/grid-and-snap#object-snap).
</Callout>
<Callout type="info" emoji="ℹ️">
The starting and ending points can be selected at any point on the curve, edge, face, or construction plane.
</Callout>

1. Click the button <ButtonIcon type="button-bridge-curve" /> on the Toolbar.
2. Click on the starting point
   - <kbd>X</kbd> **X axis:** Limit to the X-axis direction.
   - <kbd>Y</kbd> **Y axis:** Limit to the Y-axis direction.
   - <kbd>Z</kbd> **Z axis:** Limit to the Z-axis direction.
   - <kbd>N</kbd> **Normal** Limit to the Face's normal direction.  
3. Click on the ending point.  
4. Set the options in the command dialog and Shortcut Key.
   - **T:**
        - **Value 1:** The relative position (0~1.0) on the segment of the starting point.
        - **Value 2:** The relative position (0~1.0) on the segment of the ending point.
   - **Direction:** 
        - **Direction 1**: Reverse the direction of the starting point.
        - **Direction 2**: Reverse the direction of the ending point.
   - **Tension 1:**
        - **Value 1:** The tension value(\<0,0\<) of the starting point.
        - **Value 2:** The tension value(\<0,0\<) of the ending point.
   - **Tension 2:**
        - **Value 1:** 
        - **Value 2:**
   - **Tension 3:**
        - **Value 1:**
        - **Value 2:**
   - **Start curvature:**
        - **G0, G1, G2, G3:** Specifies the [continuity of the curve](../cad-essentials/continuity-curve-and-surface) at the starting point.
     -  **End curvature:**
        - **G0, G1, G2, G3:** Specifies the [continuity of the curve](../cad-essentials/continuity-curve-and-surface) at the ending point.      
   - **Trim:** When turned on, any curve that extends beyond the bridge will be trimmed.
4. Confirm with OK in the command dialog or right click.

## Bridge Edge[#bridge-edge]

1. Select two Edges you want to bridge.
2. Use **"Bridge"** in the Command Palette
3. Set the options in the command dialog and Shortcut Key.
   - **Side:** 
        - **Side 1:** Specifies which side of the endpoint of the initially selected edge to bridge to.
        - **Side 2:** Specifies which side of the endpoint of the subsequently selected edge to bridge to.
   - **Tension 1:**
        - **Value 1:** The tension value(\<0,0\<) of the starting point.
        - **Value 2:** The tension value(\<0,0\<) of the ending point.
   - **Tension 2:**
        - **Value 1:** 
        - **Value 2:**
   - **Tension 3:**
        - **Value 1:** 
        - **Value 2:**
   - **Start curvature:**
        - **G0, G1, G2, G3:** Specifies the [continuity of the curve](../cad-essentials/continuity-curve-and-surface) at the starting point.
     -  **End curvature:**
        - **G0, G1, G2, G3:** Specifies the [continuity of the curve](../cad-essentials/continuity-curve-and-surface) at the ending point.    
4. Confirm with OK in the command dialog or right click.
